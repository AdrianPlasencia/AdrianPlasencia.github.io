      <p>
        Presentation for the 14th Colloquium of Economics Students at PUCP, based on the paper of the same name. This study analyzes the association between railway system investment and economic activity in historical Peru between 1840 and 1890. The paper examines theoretical and empirical literature alongside historical data, defining the railway system and real GDP growth as core variables. Using data from 1867 to 1877, a simple correlation methodology is applied to evaluate the relationship between track expansion and economic performance. The findings indicate that while railway investment exhibited a positive association with economic activity, this relationship was heavily conditioned by exogenous factors such as dependence on extractive resources, institutional weakness, corruption, and insufficient regional connectivity.<br>
